Dr. Manuel Lequerica Tamara

Associate Lecturer at the University of Sydney

Specializing in urban ecology and biodiversity conservation, with a focus on insect communities and plant-pollinator interactions in urban environments.

Current Research

Urban Pollinator Conservation

Research with NCEAS developing data-driven approaches to identify native plants that support urban pollinator communities.

Forest Restoration Ecology

Collaboration with LEBTYP examining seed dispersal mechanisms and species turnover in Colombian ecosystems.

Eastern Suburbs Banksia Scrub

Investigation of floral visitation patterns in this Critically Endangered Ecosystem in Sydney.

Citizen Science

Analysis of socio-economic dimensions influencing the use of citizen science applications.
